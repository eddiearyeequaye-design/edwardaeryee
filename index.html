★  Flagship Project — 2026
Subsea VFM Pro
Hybrid Physics-ML Virtual Flow Meter · Live Deployment
✓  Production Deployed

Subsea flow meters are among the most expensive, maintenance-intensive instruments in offshore production — and when they fail, operators are left guessing at flowrates. VFM Pro addresses this directly: instead of a hardware sensor, it uses a computational model that combines Beggs-Brill multiphase physics with a bootstrap gradient boosting ensemble to infer liquid, gas, and water flowrates from upstream pressure and temperature data alone.

The hybrid architecture matters. Pure ML models drift when production conditions shift outside the training distribution. Pure physics models break when the correlation assumptions no longer hold. Combining both produces a model that is both accurate and physically consistent — validated at 6.1% MAPE across 5,500+ daily production records from Equinor's open-source Volve field dataset. Deployed as a full-stack application with live sensor integration and automated batch processing.

Geostatistics · 2024
GeoStat Pro

Reservoir characterisation from sparse well data is fundamentally an interpolation problem — and the method you choose determines how much uncertainty you carry into every subsequent decision. GeoStat Pro implements three spatial estimation techniques (Ordinary Kriging, Sequential Gaussian Simulation, and Inverse Distance Weighting) side by side, letting engineers compare outputs and understand what each method assumes. Built as a deployed web app, not just a script — so the analysis is accessible without a Python environment.

CFD · 2025
Slug Flow Prediction Model

Slug flow — the intermittent surging of gas pockets through liquid-filled pipes — is one of the most damaging and least predictable phenomena in subsea production. Left uncontrolled, it causes pressure surges, separator flooding, and structural fatigue. This project built a COMSOL Multiphysics two-phase flow model to predict Taylor bubble length and frequency in vertical pipes, giving designers quantitative tools to anticipate slug behaviour before it becomes a field problem. Team of five; I led the modelling approach and correlation development.

Numerical Simulation · 2025
Reservoir Pressure Mini-Simulator

Commercial reservoir simulators are powerful but opaque — you put inputs in and results come out, with little visibility into the numerical machinery underneath. This simulator was built from first principles: a finite-difference scheme on a variable Cartesian grid, solving the pressure diffusivity equation iteratively. The goal wasn't to compete with Eclipse — it was to demonstrate that the governing physics are tractable in code, and to build an intuition for how grid resolution, time-stepping, and boundary conditions affect results. Deployed as an interactive app so the model is explorable.

Process Engineering · 2025
Automated Separator Sizing Tool

Separator sizing in early-stage facility design typically involves iterative hand calculations — applying API and retention time criteria, checking droplet settling velocities, iterating on vessel dimensions. It's not intellectually demanding, but it's time-consuming and error-prone when done manually under deadline pressure. This tool takes production rates, GOR, and operating conditions as inputs and outputs vessel dimensions, retention times, and a sizing summary — automating the routine so engineers can focus on the decisions that actually require judgment.
